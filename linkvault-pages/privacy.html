Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026

🔒

The short version: we collect nothing.

LinkVault does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal data. There are no accounts, no analytics, no crash reporting, and no telemetry of any kind. Everything you do in the app stays on your device.

Data We Collect

None. LinkVault does not collect any personal information, usage data, or device identifiers. The app has no backend server, no database, and no way to receive data from your device.

Network Activity

The only network requests LinkVault makes are ones you directly trigger:

  • Downloading a media file from a link you saved
  • Scraping metadata (title, artist, thumbnail) from a YouTube link you shared into the app

These requests go directly to the relevant platform (e.g. YouTube). No data passes through any server owned or operated by this project.

Permissions

LinkVault requests only the permissions it needs to function:

Permission Why it's needed
Internet Downloading media and scraping metadata from links you save
Read / Write Storage Saving downloaded files to your device storage
Foreground Service Keeping downloads running while the app is in the background

LinkVault does not request access to your contacts, location, microphone, camera, or any other sensitive device feature.

Third Party Services

LinkVault uses yt-dlp internally to process downloads. yt-dlp is an open source tool that runs entirely on your device — it does not send data to any third party service. You can review its source code at github.com/yt-dlp/yt-dlp.

Children's Privacy

LinkVault does not collect data from anyone, including children. Since no data is collected at all, there is nothing additional to address here.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. Given that the app collects no data, significant changes are unlikely.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Open an issue on GitHub.
